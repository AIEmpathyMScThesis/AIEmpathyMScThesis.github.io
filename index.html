Abstract

This research integrates computational neuroscience and artificial intelligence to model affective empathy through three synergistic studies. First, we implement emotion detection for SemEval 2025 Task 11 by combining BERT contextual embeddings with EmoLex affective lexicons and threshold-optimised multi-label classification, achieving competitive performance (Macro F1: 0.564) in multi-label emotion recognition. Second, inspired by the anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)'s role in affective pain processing, we propose an ``Artificial Pain" framework using multi-task MentalRoBERTa to jointly classify emotions and predict their intensity (1-5 scale) in emotion support dialogues. This approach significantly outperforms baselines in emotion classification (F1: 0.628); while capturing intensity dynamics. Third, we simulate mirror/anti-mirror neuronal activity from empathy experiments using inhomogeneous Poisson processes with Gaussian-modulated firing rates. Our in silico model replicates key neurobiological findings---including higher anti-mirror neuron prevalence in the insular cortex (InC) versus ACC---validating this approach for probing empathy circuits without invasive recordings. Collectively, we demonstrate: (1) Enhanced emotion recognition through hybrid feature representations; (2) Neuroscientifically grounded emotion intensity encoding; and (3) Computational replication of empathy-related neural substrates. This work establishes pathways for AI systems to internalise emotional states, advancing beyond cognitive empathy toward affective resonance.
