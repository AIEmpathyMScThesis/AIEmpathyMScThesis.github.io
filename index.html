Abstract

Current chatbots excel at demonstrating cognitive empathy through language analysis but they lack mechanisms to internalize emotional intensity, a hallmark of human affective empathy mediated by neurobiological substrates like the anterior cingulate cortex (ACC). We propose a framework inspired by ACC-mediated "artificial pain" encoding, integrating emotion classification with intensity regression. Evaluating MentalBERT, MentalRoBERTa, and ModernBERT on the Emotional Support Conversations (ESConv) dataset, we implement multi-task learning for joint categorization of seven emotion classes and emotion intensity prediction (1â€“5 scale). MentalRoBERTa achieves state-of-the-art performance in single-task classification (F1=0.5881) and multi-task settings (F1=0.6279), with intensity regression showing significant correlations to the human-annotated ground-truth, but with relatively high estimation error. While multi-task learning improves emotion classification through shared intensity signals, predicting the intensity of emotions remains challenging, highlighting the need for model training with larger datasets. This work establishes a benchmark for emotion intensity-aware affective AI, bridging natural language processing methods with neuroscientific principles, with future implications to advance affective empathy in human-agent interactions.
